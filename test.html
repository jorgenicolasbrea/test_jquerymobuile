Course description

This research-oriented graduate course focuses study algorithms for graph partitioning and clustering, constructions of expander graphs, and analysis of random walks. These are three topics that build on the same mathematical background and that have several important connections: for example it is possible to find graph clusters via random walks, and it is possible to use the linear programming approach to graph partitioning as a way to study random walks.

Assignment

Problem 1.1:
Let L be the Laplacian matrix of a regular undirected graph G=(V,E), and let 0=λ1≤λ2≤⋯≤λn be the eigenvalues of L in non-decreasing order, counted with multiplicities, where n=|V| is the number of vertices. Suppose that λ2=0 and λk>0 for some k≥3. Let also x be an eigenvector of λ2.
Prove that the entries of x take at most k−1 distinct values.

Problem 1.2:
Let L be the Laplacian matrix of a regular undirected graph G=(V,E), and let 0=λ1≤λ2≤⋯≤λn be the eigenvalues of L in non-decreasing order, counted with multiplicities, where n=|V| is the number of vertices. Suppose that λn−1=2.
Prove that G is disconnected.
